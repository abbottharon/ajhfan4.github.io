Enhancing Real-Time Business Performance Management with Intelligent Bots

Introduction

In today's fast-paced business landscape, staying competitive and efficient is paramount. One way to achieve this is by harnessing the power of intelligent bots, which have evolved significantly since their introduction. This article explores how intelligent bots, similar to CA Unicenter's 'Neugents,' can enhance real-time business performance management and reporting. We will delve into various aspects of these bots, including their role in customer retention, their application in different industries, and their impact on financial markets. Additionally, we'll discuss the differences and similarities between operational and tactical dashboards and their relevance in modern business management.

The Cost of Customer Acquisition vs. Retention

Customer acquisition is a costly endeavor, with studies showing that acquiring a new consumer for a specific service costs five times more than retaining a current consumer [1]. In this context, intelligent bots play a crucial role in retaining customers by assisting them in making informed decisions and providing high-quality recommendations. These bots leverage online consumer behavior tracking and data acquisition to offer customers the most relevant and stimulating products, thereby increasing sales for online retailers and fostering customer loyalty [1].

While there is a wealth of literature on intelligent bots, many of these studies have focused on improving their effectiveness and efficiency as sales tools. However, there is untapped potential in utilizing intelligent bots for management and reporting purposes. Several online stores have already experimented with different intelligent bot techniques to enhance return on investment, shifting their focus from merely selling products to providing a comprehensive customer experience [1]. Notably, intelligent bots are also employed on popular websites like Wikipedia, where they serve as content management and monitoring tools, automating repetitive tasks and improving overall management [2].

Intelligent Bots in Financial Markets

The application of intelligent bots extends beyond the realm of e-commerce and content management. Salmon and Stokes [3] discuss the use of smart bots in predicting stock market behavior. These bots leverage cleverly designed algorithms to gain a competitive advantage in trading. In some instances, these algorithms have been so effective that regulatory bodies like the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and the Securities and Exchange Commission have released reports on the topic.

One notable case highlighted by Salmon and Stokes [3] involves a large fundamental trader who used an algorithm to hedge its stock market position. This trade, executed within a remarkably aggressive 20-minute timeframe, triggered a market drop as other algorithms reacted to it. However, the trader was eventually exposed thanks to the bots employed by stock markets. These bots monitored the offending algorithms and generated the necessary reports to implicate the trader [3]. This example illustrates the critical role of intelligent bots in financial market surveillance and management.

Operational vs. Tactical Dashboards: Differences and Similarities

Dashboards are indispensable tools in modern business management. Gitlow [4] introduces the concept of organizational dashboards, which serve as instruments for management to align their actions with the organization's mission statement. Organizational dashboards can be categorized as both operational and tactical. Here, we explore the differences and similarities between these two types of dashboards.

Operational dashboards, as described by Gitlow, are akin to an automotive dashboard. They provide real-time measurements to assess whether the organization is on track in terms of performance. These dashboards support managing by process, focusing on day-to-day operations and ensuring that performance aligns with objectives. Operational dashboards are characterized by simplicity, minimal coaching requirements, and straightforward reporting [4].

In contrast, tactical dashboards are future-oriented. They set targets, utilize what-if analysis, and identify trends and opportunities. Tactical dashboards enable organizations to transform strategic goals into actionable initiatives. While operational dashboards monitor current performance, tactical dashboards help plan for the future, requiring greater drill-down capabilities and providing a condensed presentation of data [4].

Viaene and Willems [5] emphasize that operational dashboards should provide detailed information, minimizing the need for drill-downs. These dashboards are designed for employees who are actively engaged in day-to-day operations and have limited time to analyze complex data. On the other hand, tactical dashboards are more analytical and require deeper exploration of data to identify trends and make informed decisions. The intersection of operational and tactical dashboards offers a unique opportunity for organizations to gain a competitive edge by effectively combining real-time monitoring with future-oriented planning [5].

Conclusion

Intelligent bots have emerged as invaluable tools for enhancing real-time business performance management and reporting. Their applications extend across various industries, from e-commerce and content management to financial markets. As demonstrated by case studies and research, these bots play a pivotal role in customer retention, operational efficiency, and even market surveillance.

In parallel, the distinction between operational and tactical dashboards highlights the importance of balancing real-time monitoring with future-oriented planning in modern business management. Leveraging both types of dashboards can empower organizations to make informed decisions, adapt to changing market conditions, and maintain a competitive edge.

In conclusion, intelligent bots and dashboard systems represent crucial components of modern business strategies. As technology continues to evolve, their role in enhancing performance management and reporting will only become more prominent, offering businesses the tools they need to thrive in an increasingly dynamic and competitive environment.
